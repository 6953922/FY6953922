Conference paper: How Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Improves Ship Design

Abstract

When engineers design a ship, they need to make sure it moves smoothly through water without wasting fuel or becoming unstable. But testing ship designs physically (in water tanks) is expensive and slow. So instead, they use CFD—a computer simulation that shows how water and air move around a ship's hull or propeller. Engineers can test hundreds of designs digitally before building anything in real life.

Introduction

Around 60–70% of a ship's fuel is used just to overcome water resistance (drag). Even a 1% reduction can save thousands of tonnes of fuel yearly. CFD helps design more efficient ships—less fuel, less pollution, and lower costs.
